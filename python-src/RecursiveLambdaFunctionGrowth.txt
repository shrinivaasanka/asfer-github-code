The State Government issued a Government Order giving force to its proposal to expand the Chennai Metropolitan Planning Area by adding villages from three nearby districts â€” Tiruvallur, Kancheepuram, and Vellore.  In the G.O. issued by the Housing and Urban Development department on January 22, 2018, the government has expanded the jurisdiction of the Chennai Metropolitan Planning Area.  This widening was carried out based on Demand no. 26 moved by the Housing and Urban Development Minister in the Legislative Assembly on July 3, 2017.  As part of the expansion, 1,709 villages have been added to the Chennai Metropolitan area.  From Tiruvallur district, villages of the Ponneri, Gummidipoondi, Uthukottai, Tiruvallur, Tiruttani and Pallipattu taluks have been included.  From Kancheepuram district, villages of the Kancheepuram, Walajabad, Sriperumbudur, Chengalpet, Tiruporur, Uthiramerur, Madurantakam, Thirukazhukundram, and Cheyyur taluks have been included, while those from the Arakkonam and Nemili taluks have been added in the list from Vellore district.  The G.O. also directs the Collectors of Tiruvallur, Kancheepuram and Vellore districts to republic the notification in their respective District Gazettes.
